Innovation Challenge 1 — UC MarketHub

During Innovation Challenge 1, I worked with three classmates to pitch UC MarketHub, a student-focused marketplace for textbooks, lab gear, and dorm items. We started by talking to friends and clubmates about how much unused stuff piles up at semester’s end, then drafted a simple “problem / solution” slide to confirm the pain point.

My main jobs were outlining the core features — verified UC e-mail login, quick photo listing, and a rating system — and turning those ideas into a rough Figma mock-up for our final pitch. The challenge taught me the nuts and bolts of design thinking: scoping an MVP, gathering quick feedback, and translating that into a clear story for judges.

Presenting the deck also gave me a first real taste of public speaking in front of faculty and peers. Most importantly, it showed how engineering ideas intersect with business viability, nudging me to explore a future minor in Business Analytics alongside my mechanical-engineering work.

Exploring Culture, Architecture and Urban Life in Chicago.

During my self designed Honors experience, I traveled to Chicago to explore how architecture, culture, and public spaces shape the identity of a major city. Over five days, I visited locations such as Navy Pier, Millennium Park, the Magnificent Mile, the Griffin Museum of Science and Industry, Adler Planetarium, and the University of Chicago.

I also experienced cultural environments including Chinatown, the German Christmas Market, and the Thanksgiving Parade. Through structured observation, journaling, and photography, I studied how different communities express identity through architecture, traditions, and shared public spaces.

One of the most interesting parts of the experience was learning how Chicago’s architecture reflects both creativity and engineering. I observed how skyscrapers are designed with structural features to manage strong winds coming from Lake Michigan and how the Chicago Loop supports the movement of millions of people each day.

This experience strengthened my cultural awareness and helped me see cities as dynamic environments where design, history, and diverse communities interact. My collection of observation notes and photographs highlights how architecture and public spaces contribute to Chicago’s identity.
